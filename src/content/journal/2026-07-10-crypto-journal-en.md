## Recap

The session saw a high volume of closed trades, totaling 55 executions. The overall performance metrics indicate a strong positive expectancy, calculated at +1.27% per trade, supported by a profit factor of 11.76. The win rate registered at 78.2%, which is quite robust. The asymmetry between average wins (+1.78%) and average losses (-0.54%) contributed significantly to the positive outcome. The notable gains in assets like Particle Network (PARTI) and Golem (GLM) provided significant positive anchors for the period. <img class="emoji-char" src="/characters/crypto/09_excited.png" alt="Bengal" />

## Analysis of Outcomes

The recorded losses, such as those in ThetaDrop (TDROP) and Fanc (FANC), highlight instances where the downside capture was more pronounced than the average loss suggests. The best recorded return of +4.65% on PARTI demonstrates the potential magnitude when directional conviction aligns with market momentum. The positive skew observed across the closed trades suggests that the risk management framework successfully limited downside exposure relative to the upside capture. <img class="emoji-char" src="/characters/crypto/07_confident.png" alt="Bengal" />

## Lessons from Underperformance

Reviewing the losing trades, particularly those with smaller negative captures, suggests that the market structure in those specific instances was resistant to the initial thesis. The depth of the drawdowns, even when small in percentage terms, reinforces the need to treat the initial entry conviction with caution when the immediate follow-through is absent. It is clear that the market's immediate rejection of the thesis in those instances warrants a more measured approach to entry confirmation. <img class="emoji-char" src="/characters/crypto/11_thinking.png" alt="Bengal" />
