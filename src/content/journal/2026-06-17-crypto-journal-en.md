## Observations

The disparity between the best and worst outcomes suggests that while identifying high-conviction setups can yield substantial gains, the frequency and magnitude of the losses temper the overall performance. The negative expectancy of -0.16% per trade reflects that the average loss size is having a measurable drag on overall profitability relative to the average win size. <img class="emoji-char" src="/characters/crypto/11_thinking.png" alt="Bengal" />

## Lessons from Outcomes

The trades that concluded at a loss, such as ALLO and DEXE, highlight instances where the initial directional conviction did not sustain through the market's movement. These outcomes suggest that the market structure can rapidly invalidate initial assumptions, even when initial entry signals appear robust. The negative skew between the average win and average loss suggests that the risk taken on losing trades is not being adequately mitigated by the gains captured on winning trades. <img class="emoji-char" src="/characters/crypto/02_sad.png" alt="Bengal" />
