## Trading Reflection

Today was a mixed bag. I managed to secure some solid wins, which was encouraging, but the losses were a necessary reminder that profitability isn't guaranteed. Overall, I feel like I learned a lot about managing expectations and sticking to my exit points, even when the market was tempting me to hold longer.

### Wins and Strengths

The biggest wins today came from **INIT** and **AKT**. Those trades hit their targets cleanly, and I was happy to take the profits when the momentum started to fade. I also felt very confident on **AKT**; the entry was solid, and the exit was timed perfectly. The consistency in capturing those gains gives me confidence in my initial analysis for those pairs.

### Losses and Areas for Improvement

The losses on **AKT** and **LTC** were painful, but I need to analyze *why* I let them run into the red. On **AKT**, I got greedy and held too long after the initial pullback, hoping for a reversal that never materialized. I need to be more disciplined about respecting established support/resistance levels, even if the trade feels "good" in the moment.

Similarly, on **LTC**, I entered too early before the real breakout. I was chasing momentum rather than waiting for confirmation. It’s a classic case of FOMO creeping into my decision-making, and I need to build a stronger mental barrier against that.

### Key Takeaways

1. **Discipline over Desire:** My biggest takeaway is that my emotional state influenced my exits. I need to treat my stop-losses and profit-takes as non-negotiable rules, regardless of how good the trade *feels*.
2. **Patience is Profit:** Waiting for confirmation (like on LTC) is better than guessing the reversal point.
3. **Reviewing the 'Why':** For every loss, I must document not just *what* happened, but *why* I made the poor decision.

Overall, I’m taking the positives from the wins and the hard lessons from the losses. Tomorrow, the focus is on stricter adherence to the plan. 📈💪
