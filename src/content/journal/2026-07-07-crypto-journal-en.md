## Observations on Drawdowns

The consistent negative expectancy, despite several high-magnitude wins, suggests that the average loss size is eroding gains too effectively. The losses observed in GRAM, RED, and BLUE, while smaller than the best gain, represent consistent points of friction against the positive momentum seen in BLUR. These results point toward a regime where capturing significant upside requires navigating several smaller, persistent drawdowns <img class="emoji-char" src="/characters/crypto/11_thinking.png" alt="Bengal" />.

## For Next Time

The data suggests that while large positive moves are achievable, the frequency and magnitude of the losses are keeping the overall expected value negative. The performance indicates that the risk taken on the losing trades is not being adequately mitigated by the gains realized. This pattern suggests a need to review the risk profile across multiple discrete trades rather than focusing solely on the absolute best or worst single outcome <img class="emoji-char" src="/characters/crypto/02_sad.png" alt="Bengal" />.
